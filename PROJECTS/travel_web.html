Top Attractions

  • Pagoda

    a serene body of water, its surface reflecting the vibrant hues of the surrounding landscape in Bali. In the background stands the iconic Ulun Danu Beratan Temple, a multi-tiered pagoda that appears to float gracefully over the water’s edge. This temple is nestled along Lake Beratan, one of Bali’s most breathtaking bodies of water, framed by lush green mountains and often veiled by a soft mist that adds a mystical ambiance. The pagoda, dedicated to the goddess of water, features distinct Balinese architecture, with dark, layered roofs and intricately carved stone sculptures, blending harmoniously with the natural surroundings. As the sunlight changes throughout the day, the lake and temple reflect shades of gold, emerald, and deep blue, creating a tranquil scene that embodies Bali’s deep spiritual connection to nature.

  • the Gate at the Lempuyang Temple

    The Gate of Lempuyang Temple, known as the “Gates of Heaven,” is a majestic stone gateway that perfectly frames the distant silhouette of Mount Agung, Bali’s highest and holiest volcano. This split gate, with its intricately carved patterns and towering height, opens up to breathtaking panoramic views that seem almost otherworldly. Visitors standing between the two pillars often feel as though they are stepping into another realm, with the sky reflected in the glossy stone floor below, making it a popular spot for capturing Bali’s beauty in its most mystical and awe-inspiring form.

Places to visit

  • Ubud palace

    • The palace has beautifully crafted buildings with high aesthetic value. The walls and doorways have a distinctive design carved in stone, and of course, there is also a temple inside the palace. You can wander around, explore the buildings and items, and pay heed to the details of every object of the Balinese traditional cultures there – or take some shots for your Instagram as well! The palace is also equipped with an auditorium and a banyan tree as a shelter during the daytime.

  • Waterbom

    Venture into the lush Balinese jungles for a waterfall adventure you won’t soon forget. Your private guide takes you to a number of waterfalls where you can admire the cascading waters and take a dip in the refreshing pools. You also get to enjoy a typical Balinese lunch at a local restaurant as part of your experience.

  • Mount Bature Sunrise Jeep Tour

    You don’t need to hike through the night to experience the magic of Bali’s Mt. Batur volcano. This private 4WD tour, with 2-way transfers direct from your Ubud hotel, gets you into eerie landscapes of volcanic sands and lava caves. You’ll take in island views, explore the forest, and soak in the healing waters of a natural hot spring. For ease, your package includes lunch (or brunch) and bottled water.
