Title Of Poem: Path of the Dreamer

Through twilight’s veil, the dreamer walks,
Where silence hums and shadows talk.
The stars above in whispers say,
"Dream bold, young soul—forge your way."

With every step through night’s soft maze,
Hope glimmers like a morning blaze.
The winds may shift, the roads may twine,
But still your heart beats, clear and fine.

For those who dream don’t walk alone—
The universe makes paths unknown.
In every fall, a lesson gleams,
In every loss, the birth of dreams.

So hold your head and chase the sky,
Let doubts and fears fall low, drift by.
The gift of dreams is yours to keep,
Awake or not—fly wide, or sleep.

And when the world begins to see,
The dreamer will have set it free.
